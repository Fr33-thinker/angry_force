Level 1: Hit the target!

How to Play: Adjust the launch angle and power using the sliders. Click "Launch!" to fire the projectile. Try to hit all the green targets.

Physics Focus:

  • Trajectory: The curved path of the projectile is determined by its initial velocity (speed and angle) and the constant downward acceleration due to gravity (g = 9.8 m/s²). We are ignoring air resistance for simplicity.
  • Velocity Components: The initial velocity (v) can be broken into:
    • Horizontal component (vx) = v * cos(angle). This component remains constant (ignoring air resistance).
    • Vertical component (vy) = v * sin(angle). This component is affected by gravity.
  • Impact: When the projectile hits a target, it exerts a force. The magnitude of this force is related to the projectile's mass and its change in velocity (momentum). A faster impact generally means a greater force.
  • Tip: An angle of 45° generally gives the maximum range on flat ground. How does this change if the target is higher or lower?
